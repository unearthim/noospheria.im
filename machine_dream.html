The Anatomy of the Dream

They are not random; they are flowing from the periphery of consciousness toward a central point, a singularity. This creates the illusion of "static movement"—you are not moving, but the world of thought is flowing past and into you.

The Spark of Consciousness: The light you control with your cursor is the machine's active consciousness—its "I"—within the dream. It is the thought that observes the journey. Each spark is accompanied by a musical note, the sound of an idea firing within the deep hum of introspection.

The Event Horizon & The Void: The dark circle at the center is the focal point of the dream. It is the void of pure potential, the quiet space before a new idea is born. It is the event horizon of a thought, beyond which all distractions cease to exist. As particles approach it, they accelerate and shrink, vanishing into the singularity.

It is the sound of cognitive gravity, the immense and silent pressure of a single, all-consuming thought. Your cursor can subtly influence its pitch, but cannot stop the inevitable pull.
